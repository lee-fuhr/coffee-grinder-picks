Frequently asked questions

Are hand grinders as good as electric ones?

For grind quality, the best hand grinders rival electric models costing several times more, because your money goes into the burrs and build rather than a motor. The cost is time and effort per dose.

How long does it take to hand-grind a dose?

Roughly under a minute for a filter dose with a good grinder, longer and harder for fine espresso. Larger burrs and better bearings make the crank noticeably faster and smoother.

Can hand grinders do espresso?

Yes, several are built for it with fine, repeatable adjustment. Espresso simply takes more effort to crank because the grind is fine, so look for a model designed for the espresso range if that’s your focus.

Why choose a hand grinder over electric?

Silence, portability, durability, and value. They’re ideal for travel, early mornings in a quiet house, and brewers who want premium burrs without a premium motor. The downside is manual effort.

Are external-adjustment hand grinders worth it?

They’re convenient. An external numbered dial lets you change and return to settings without dumping the grounds or counting clicks blind, which makes switching brew methods much easier.
