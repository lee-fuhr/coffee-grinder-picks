Quick answer

The Baratza Encore is the best grinder for French press because it reliably produces coarse, even grounds with minimal sludge.

The 1Zpresso JX-Pro suits travelers or anyone wanting a premium manual option, while the Baratza Virtuoso+ is ideal for filter coffee enthusiasts who demand extra consistency.

French press coffee thrives on a coarse, uniform grind. Too many fines slip through the mesh and turn your cup muddy, while uneven particles lead to bitter or sour results. The right grinder avoids both pitfalls by using quality burrs and step-less or fine-step adjustment, so you can dial in exactly the right coarseness. When choosing a grinder for French press, focus on burr type (flat or conical), adjustment range, and how consistently it produces large particles. Electric models offer speed and convenience; hand grinders give you portability and often better control for the same budget. Frequently asked questions

What grind size is best for French press?

Coarse, think coarse sea salt. The press steeps for several minutes and uses a metal mesh, so a coarse, even grind extracts properly and keeps fines out of the cup.

Why is my French press gritty?

Too many fines. Blade grinders and worn or poorly adjusted burrs produce dust that slips through the mesh. A quality burr grinder set coarse, with low fines, is the fix for a clean press.

Can I use a blade grinder for French press?

It’s the most forgiving brew method for a blade grinder, but you’ll still get unevenness and grit. A burr grinder noticeably improves clarity and body even at the coarse end.

How coarse is too coarse for French press?

If the cup tastes thin, weak, or sour, the grind is too coarse and under-extracting. Step it slightly finer until the body fills out without turning muddy or bitter.

Does grind consistency matter at coarse settings?

Yes. Even at coarse, a tight particle distribution means fewer fines clouding the cup and fewer boulders under-extracting, so the press tastes cleaner and more balanced.
